## Overview

Singapore is consistently ranked as one of the easiest places in the world to do business. A Singapore Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd) offers a low effective tax rate, no capital gains tax, an extensive double taxation treaty network, and strong rule of law. It is the premier jurisdiction for founders targeting the Asia-Pacific region and is widely regarded as one of the most credible corporate domiciles globally.

## Entity Types Available

- **Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd)** - The standard structure for most businesses. Limited to 50 shareholders. The default choice for startups and SMEs
- **Exempt Private Company (EPC)** - A Pte Ltd with no more than 20 shareholders, none of which are corporate entities. Enjoys certain audit exemptions
- **Public Company (Ltd)** - For companies intending to offer shares to the public. More onerous compliance requirements
- **Branch Office** - Extension of a foreign company. Not a separate legal entity
- **Representative Office** - For market research only. Cannot conduct business or earn revenue
- **Sole Proprietorship / Partnership** - Simpler structures but no limited liability

**The Pte Ltd is the right choice for most founders.**

## Tax Structure

Singapore has a **territorial tax system** with a headline rate of 17%, but effective rates are significantly lower due to partial exemptions and incentives.

### Corporate Income Tax

| Chargeable Income | Effective Rate | How It Works |
|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| First SGD 10,000 | 4.25% | 75% exemption on first SGD 10k |
| Next SGD 190,000 | 8.5% | 50% exemption on next SGD 190k |
| Above SGD 200,000 | 17% | Standard rate |

**Effective rate on first SGD 200,000:** approximately 8.4%

### New Company Tax Exemption (first 3 years)

| Chargeable Income | Effective Rate | How It Works |
|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| First SGD 100,000 | 4.25% | 75% exemption |
| Next SGD 100,000 | 8.5% | 50% exemption |
| Above SGD 200,000 | 17% | Standard rate |

**Key points:**

- **No capital gains tax** - gains from disposal of shares, property, or other assets are not taxed
- **No withholding tax on dividends** - Singapore does not tax dividends at any level (one-tier system)
- **Extensive treaty network** - 90+ comprehensive double taxation agreements
- **Foreign-sourced income** is taxable only when remitted to Singapore, with broad exemptions for dividends, branch profits, and service income from treaty countries
- **GST (Goods and Services Tax):** 9% standard rate. Registration required if annual taxable turnover exceeds SGD 1 million
- **Transfer pricing rules** apply - arm's length standard for related-party transactions

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Reserve a company name** with ACRA (Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority) - SGD 15, approved within hours
2. **Prepare incorporation documents:** constitution (formerly memorandum and articles of association), consent of directors, registered office address
3. **Appoint a local resident director** - at least one director must be a Singapore citizen, permanent resident, or EntrePass/Employment Pass holder. Nominee director services available ($2,000-5,000/year)
4. **Appoint a company secretary** - required within 6 months of incorporation. Must be a Singapore resident
5. **Register with ACRA** - SGD 300 registration fee
6. **Open a corporate bank account**

**Total setup cost: SGD 2,000-4,000 (approximately $1,500-3,000 USD)** including nominee director and corporate secretary for the first year.

**Timeline:** 1-2 business days for ACRA registration. Bank account opening takes 1-4 weeks.

**Minimum share capital:** SGD 1 (one dollar). Can be increased at any time.

**Important:** Singapore banks strongly prefer **in-person account opening**. While some banks offer video verification, founders should plan to visit Singapore for bank account setup. DBS has a digital onboarding option for certain company profiles, and Aspire offers fully remote onboarding.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Annual Return (AR):** Filed with ACRA within 7 months of the financial year end (5 months for listed companies). Filing fee: SGD 60
- **Annual General Meeting (AGM):** Must be held within 6 months of the financial year end (EPC exemption may apply)
- **Financial Statements:** Must be prepared in accordance with Singapore Financial Reporting Standards (SFRS)
- **Audit:** Required if annual revenue exceeds SGD 10 million, total assets exceed SGD 10 million, or employees exceed 50 (meeting any 2 of 3 criteria for 2 consecutive years). Small companies meeting the EPC criteria are exempt
- **Corporate Tax Filing:** Estimated Chargeable Income (ECI) due within 3 months of the financial year end. Form C-S (simplified) or Form C due by November 30
- **GST Returns:** Quarterly if GST-registered
- **Company Secretary:** Must maintain a qualified company secretary at all times
- **Registered Office:** Must maintain a local registered office address accessible during business hours

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | Low effective tax rate - approximately 8.4% on the first SGD 200,000 of profits |
| Pro | No capital gains tax and no withholding tax on dividends |
| Pro | Extensive double taxation treaty network with 90+ countries |
| Pro | World-class reputation and credibility - consistently top-ranked for ease of doing business |
| Pro | Stable political environment, strong rule of law, and IP protection |
| Pro | Strategic location for Asia-Pacific market access |
| Con | Local resident director requirement adds cost ($2,000-5,000/year for nominee) |
| Con | In-person bank account opening is strongly preferred by most banks |
| Con | Cost of living and office space in Singapore is high if you need physical presence |
| Con | Audit requirements can be expensive for larger companies |
| Con | GST registration threshold (SGD 1M) means most small companies are exempt but lose input tax recovery |
| Con | Annual compliance costs are higher than simpler jurisdictions like Wyoming or Estonia |

## Best For

Singapore is ideal for **founders targeting the Asia-Pacific market, companies holding intellectual property, and businesses that benefit from a credible and well-regulated jurisdiction**. It works particularly well for SaaS companies with APAC customers, e-commerce businesses, IP holding structures, and companies that need access to Southeast Asian markets. The combination of low effective tax rates, no capital gains tax, and an extensive treaty network makes it a strong choice for scaling businesses with international operations.
