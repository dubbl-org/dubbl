## Overview

Estonia's e-Residency program is a government-issued digital identity that allows non-Estonians to start and manage an EU-based company entirely online. Combined with the OÜ (osaühing - private limited company) structure and Estonia's unique tax system that taxes corporate profits only upon distribution, it has become one of the most popular options for digital nomads, remote workers, and location-independent founders who want an EU company without physically relocating.

## Entity Types Available

- **OÜ (Osaühing)** - Private limited company. The standard and most common structure for e-residents. Minimum share capital of €0.01 (though €2,500 is the traditional amount - it can be left unpaid initially)
- **AS (Aktsiaselts)** - Public limited company. Minimum €25,000 share capital. Rarely used by e-residents
- **FIE (Füüsilisest isikust ettevotja)** - Sole proprietor. Not available to e-residents - requires Estonian residency

**The OÜ is the right choice for virtually all e-residents.**

## Tax Structure

Estonia has a unique corporate tax system: **0% tax on retained earnings, 20% tax on distributed profits.**

| Item | Rate | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Corporate income tax (retained) | 0% | No tax on undistributed profits |
| Corporate income tax (distributed) | 20/80 on net distribution | Effective 20% on gross amount |
| Regular dividend distributions | 14/86 reduced rate | Available after 3 years of regular distributions |
| VAT (standard) | 22% | Increased from 20% in 2024 |
| VAT (reduced) | 9% / 5% | Certain goods and services |
| Social tax (on salary) | 33% | Employer's contribution on Estonian payroll |
| Personal income tax | 20% | Flat rate on salary and dividends for Estonian tax residents |

**Key points:**

- **Retained earnings are completely tax-free.** If your company earns €100,000 and you reinvest it all, you pay €0 in Estonian corporate tax
- Tax is triggered only when you distribute dividends, pay fringe benefits, or make non-business expenses
- The 20/80 rule means: on a €80 net dividend, the company pays €20 tax (total gross = €100, effective rate = 20%)
- After distributing dividends regularly for 3+ years, the rate drops to 14/86 on amounts up to the average of the prior 3 years
- **No withholding tax** on dividends paid to non-resident shareholders (under domestic law - treaty provisions may apply)
- Estonia has an extensive **double taxation treaty network** as an EU member

## Setup Process and Costs

### Step 1: Apply for e-Residency

1. Apply online at e-resident.gov.ee
2. Pay the application fee: **€190**
3. Wait for processing: 3-8 weeks
4. Pick up your e-Residency card at an Estonian embassy or pickup point worldwide

### Step 2: Form an OÜ

1. **Choose a service provider** (see below) or use the Business Register directly
2. **Register the company** via the Estonian Business Register (e-business register) using your e-Residency digital ID
3. **Provide required details:** company name, registered address (typically via service provider), share capital, board member(s)
4. **Pay formation costs:** ~€200-500 via service provider, or €265 state fee if filing independently
5. **Open a business bank account**

**Total setup cost: ~€590-1,100** (€190 e-Residency + €400-900 company formation and first year of service)

**Timeline:** e-Residency card takes 3-8 weeks. Company formation takes 1-3 business days once you have the card.

| Bank | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Wise Business | Multi-currency | Most popular with e-residents, easy onboarding, multi-currency IBAN |
| LHV Pank | Estonian bank | Traditional Estonian bank, accepts e-resident companies |
| Swedbank | Estonian bank | May require in-person visit or video call |
| SEB | Estonian bank | Available but onboarding can be slower for non-residents |
| Revolut Business | Neobank | EU-licensed, accepted for Estonian companies |
| Payoneer | Payment platform | Alternative for receiving international payments |

**For e-residents:** Wise Business is the de facto standard. LHV is the most e-resident-friendly traditional bank. Other Estonian banks have tightened onboarding requirements for non-resident companies.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Annual report:** Filed digitally through the Business Register by June 30 each year (for the previous calendar year). Includes balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement
- **Tax declarations:** Monthly TSD declaration if paying salaries or dividends. Monthly VAT return if VAT-registered
- **VAT registration:** Required if taxable turnover exceeds €40,000 in a calendar year. Voluntary registration available below this threshold. Reverse charge applies for B2B EU services
- **Accounting:** Must maintain proper bookkeeping records. Most e-residents outsource this to their service provider
- **Board member KYC:** Banks and the Business Register may periodically request updated identification documents
- **Registered address:** Must maintain a valid Estonian registered address at all times

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | 0% corporate tax on retained earnings - reinvest profits without any tax drag |
| Pro | Fully digital company management - sign documents, file reports, and manage the company from anywhere |
| Pro | EU company status - access to EU single market, SEPA payments, and EU regulatory frameworks |
| Pro | Transparent and efficient digital government infrastructure |
| Pro | Growing ecosystem of service providers tailored to e-residents |
| Pro | No minimum capital requirement in practice (€0.01 minimum) |
| Con | Service provider costs add up - €1,000-1,500/year minimum for accounting and address |
| Con | Estonian bank account opening has become more difficult for non-residents |
| Con | e-Residency card requires physical pickup at an embassy |
| Con | 33% social tax on salaries makes Estonian payroll expensive |
| Con | Not a tax residency - you still owe taxes in your country of personal tax residence |
| Con | 22% VAT rate is relatively high |

## Best For

Estonia's e-Residency and OÜ structure is ideal for **digital nomads, remote freelancers, and location-independent founders** who want a legitimate EU company without relocating. It is particularly strong for businesses that retain and reinvest most of their profits, SaaS companies selling to EU customers, and founders who value the ability to manage their company entirely online. The 0% tax on retained earnings makes it especially attractive for bootstrapped businesses focused on growth over distributions.
