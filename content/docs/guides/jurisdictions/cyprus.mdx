## Overview

Cyprus is an EU member state offering one of the lowest corporate tax rates in the European Union at 12.5%, combined with a powerful IP Box regime, no withholding tax on outbound dividends, and a favorable non-domicile (non-dom) regime for individuals. Its geographic position at the crossroads of Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, along with a common law legal system inherited from British rule, makes it an attractive jurisdiction for holding companies, IP-driven businesses, and international trading operations. The 60-day tax residency rule makes it one of the easiest countries in the world for establishing personal tax residence.

## Entity Types Available

- **Private Company Limited by Shares (Ltd)** - The most common structure, requires minimum 1 director and 1 shareholder. No maximum number of shareholders (capped at 50 for private companies)
- **Public Company (PLC)** - For companies seeking public listing, minimum 7 shareholders and EUR 25,629 share capital
- **Branch of a Foreign Company** - Registered extension of a foreign entity, taxed on Cyprus-sourced income
- **European Company (SE)** - Available under EU regulation for pan-European operations

## Tax Structure

| Item | Rate |
|------|------|
| Corporate income tax (CIT) | 12.5% |
| IP Box regime (qualifying IP income) | Effective 2.5% |
| Dividend income (from qualifying participations) | 0% exempt |
| Capital gains tax | 0% (except on immovable property in Cyprus) |
| Withholding tax on dividends (to non-residents) | 0% |
| Withholding tax on interest (to non-residents) | 0% |
| Withholding tax on royalties (to non-residents) | 0% (5%/10% in some cases) |
| VAT (standard rate) | 19% |
| Special Defence Contribution (SDC) on dividends | 17% (but non-doms exempt) |

**Key points:**

- The 12.5% CIT rate applies to worldwide income of Cyprus tax resident companies
- The IP Box regime provides an effective rate as low as 2.5% on qualifying profits from IP assets (patents, copyrighted software, and other qualifying IP). Based on the OECD nexus approach
- Participation exemption: dividends received from subsidiaries are generally exempt from tax (no minimum holding period or percentage, subject to conditions)
- No withholding tax on dividends, interest, or royalties paid to non-residents (with limited exceptions), making Cyprus highly attractive for holding and IP structures
- Notional Interest Deduction (NID): companies can deduct a notional interest on new equity introduced into the business, reducing the effective tax rate further
- The 60-day tax residency rule: an individual can become a Cyprus tax resident by spending just 60 days in Cyprus (subject to conditions including not being tax resident elsewhere, having a permanent home, and conducting business in Cyprus)
- Non-domicile (non-dom) regime: individuals who are tax resident but not domiciled in Cyprus are exempt from SDC on dividends, interest, and rental income - effectively 0% tax on passive income for up to 17 years
- Extensive double tax treaty network with 65+ countries
- Group loss relief available between Cyprus resident group companies

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Name approval** - Submit proposed company name to the Registrar of Companies for approval (1-2 days)
2. **Prepare incorporation documents** - Memorandum and Articles of Association
3. **File with the Registrar of Companies** - Submit the Memorandum, Articles, and required forms (HE1, HE2, HE3)
4. **Receive Certificate of Incorporation** and company registration number
5. **Register for tax** with the Tax Department and obtain a Tax Identification Code (TIC)
6. **Register for VAT** if applicable (mandatory above EUR 15,600 turnover threshold)
7. **Open a bank account**

**Recommended share capital:** EUR 1,000 (no legal minimum, but EUR 1,000 is standard practice for credibility)

**Total setup cost: $2,000-4,000** including government fees, legal and accounting fees for incorporation, and registered office. Government fees alone are approximately EUR 500-800.

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for standard incorporation. Shelf companies available for faster setup.

## Banking Options

| Bank | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Bank of Cyprus | Traditional | Largest bank on the island, full range of business services |
| Hellenic Bank | Traditional | Second largest, good for SMEs, improving digital platform |
| Eurobank Cyprus | Traditional | Greek parent bank, good for companies with Greek connections |
| International banks | Various | EMIs and international banks (Wise, Airwallex) used alongside local accounts |

**Banking tips:** Cyprus banking has improved significantly since the 2013 crisis, but due diligence requirements remain stringent. Local banks require detailed business plans, source of funds documentation, and personal interviews. Account opening typically takes 2-6 weeks. Having a local director or using a professional introduction can speed up the process. Many companies maintain both a local Cyprus account and an international EMI account for day-to-day operations.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Annual return (HE32)** - Must be filed with the Registrar of Companies annually. Due within 28 days of the anniversary of incorporation
- **Audited financial statements** - All Cyprus limited companies are required to prepare and file audited financial statements, regardless of size. This is a mandatory requirement with no small company exemption
- **Corporate tax return (TD4)** - Due within 15 months of the end of the tax year (extended deadlines may apply for electronic filing)
- **Provisional tax assessment** - Two advance payments of estimated tax due on August 1 and December 31
- **VAT returns** - Quarterly
- **Payroll and social insurance** - Monthly contributions and reporting if employing staff in Cyprus
- **Beneficial ownership register** - Must be maintained and filed
- **Transfer pricing documentation** - Required for related party transactions (Summary Information Table filed with the tax return)

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | 12.5% CIT rate - one of the lowest in the EU |
| Pro | IP Box regime with effective 2.5% rate on qualifying IP income |
| Pro | 0% withholding tax on dividends, interest, and royalties to non-residents |
| Pro | Non-dom regime eliminates SDC on passive income for up to 17 years |
| Pro | 60-day tax residency rule - easiest way to become tax resident in an EU country |
| Pro | Notional Interest Deduction reduces effective tax on equity-financed businesses |
| Con | Mandatory audit requirement for all companies regardless of size adds annual costs |
| Con | Banking sector can be slow with onboarding and has heightened due diligence requirements |
| Con | Small domestic market limits local business opportunities |
| Con | Perception issues - Cyprus is sometimes viewed as an aggressive tax planning jurisdiction |
| Con | Limited local talent pool for specialized or technical roles |
| Con | SDC of 17% on dividends applies to domiciled individuals (non-dom exemption has a 17-year limit) |

## Best For

Cyprus is ideal for **IP-heavy businesses, holding companies, and entrepreneurs seeking a low-tax EU base with favorable personal tax treatment**. The combination of 12.5% CIT, 2.5% effective IP Box rate, 0% withholding on outbound payments, and the non-dom regime makes it one of the most tax-efficient EU jurisdictions available. The 60-day residency rule is particularly attractive for founders who travel frequently but want EU tax residency without spending extensive time in one location. Best suited for software companies, licensing businesses, international holding structures, and individuals seeking to combine EU membership with a favorable personal tax environment.
