## Overview

The US Limited Liability Company (LLC) is one of the most popular structures for founders worldwide. An LLC provides liability protection, operational flexibility, and - crucially for non-US residents - pass-through taxation that can result in 0% US federal tax at the entity level when properly structured. Delaware, Wyoming, and Florida are the three most common formation states.

## Entity Types Available

- **Single-Member LLC** - One owner, disregarded entity for tax purposes by default
- **Multi-Member LLC** - Two or more owners, taxed as a partnership by default
- **Series LLC** - Available in Delaware and Wyoming, allows multiple "series" (sub-LLCs) under one parent LLC, each with separate liability protection. Useful for holding multiple assets or product lines
- **LLC taxed as S-Corp or C-Corp** - Possible via IRS election (Form 8832 / Form 2553), though rarely beneficial for non-US owners

## Tax Structure

US LLCs use **pass-through taxation** by default. The LLC itself does not pay federal income tax. Instead, profits and losses flow through to the members' personal tax returns.

**For non-US residents with no US effectively connected income (ECI):**

| Item | Rate |
|------|------|
| Federal corporate tax at entity level | 0% (pass-through) |
| State income tax (DE) | 0% for out-of-state income |
| State income tax (WY) | 0% (no state income tax) |
| State income tax (FL) | 0% (no personal income tax) |
| Delaware franchise tax | ~$300/year flat fee for LLCs |
| Wyoming annual report | $60/year (or $0 if no assets in WY) |

**Key points:**

- Non-US single-member LLCs with no US ECI typically owe $0 in US federal tax
- You must still file Form 5472 and possibly Form 1120 annually
- If you have US-sourced income or US clients, withholding and ECI rules may apply
- State-level taxes vary - Delaware charges no income tax on out-of-state LLC income, Wyoming and Florida have no state income tax at all

### Delaware vs Wyoming vs Florida

| Factor | Delaware | Wyoming | Florida |
|--------|----------|---------|---------|
| Formation cost | ~$90 state fee | ~$100 state fee | ~$125 state fee |
| Annual cost | ~$300 franchise tax | $60 annual report | $138.75 annual report |
| Privacy | Moderate (members not public) | Strong (no public disclosure) | Moderate |
| Legal system | Court of Chancery (specialized) | Standard courts | Standard courts |
| Series LLC | Yes | Yes | No |
| State income tax | None on out-of-state income | None | None (personal) |

**Delaware** is the gold standard for venture-backed companies due to its Court of Chancery, a dedicated business court with deep precedent. **Wyoming** is the cheapest option with the strongest privacy protections. **Florida** is popular for US-based founders living in-state.

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Choose a registered agent** in your formation state ($50-200/year)
2. **File Articles of Organization** with the state ($90-150 state fee)
3. **Draft an Operating Agreement** (template or attorney, $0-500)
4. **Obtain an EIN** (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS - free, but non-US applicants must apply by fax or phone (Form SS-4)
5. **Open a US bank account**

**Total setup cost: $200-1,500** depending on whether you use a formation service or attorney.

**Timeline:** Same-day to 2 weeks depending on state and method. Expedited processing available in all three states.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Delaware:** Franchise tax ($300 flat for LLCs) due June 1 each year
- **Wyoming:** Annual report ($60 minimum) due on the anniversary of formation
- **Florida:** Annual report ($138.75) due May 1 each year
- **IRS Form 5472 + pro-forma 1120:** Required for foreign-owned single-member LLCs, due April 15 (extensions available)
- **Registered agent:** Must maintain a registered agent in the formation state at all times
- **Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI):** File with FinCEN within 90 days of formation (new requirement)

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | Pass-through taxation - 0% entity-level tax for non-US residents with no ECI |
| Pro | Flexible management structure - no board of directors required |
| Pro | Strong liability protection separating personal and business assets |
| Pro | Wide acceptance - US LLCs are recognized by payment processors, banks, and partners globally |
| Pro | Series LLC option for multi-asset or multi-product businesses |
| Con | EIN process for non-US residents requires fax or phone - can take 4-8 weeks |
| Con | US banking can be difficult to access remotely |
| Con | Annual compliance filings required even with zero revenue |
| Con | Venture capital investors often prefer C-Corps over LLCs |
| Con | State franchise taxes and registered agent fees add ongoing costs |

## Best For

US LLCs are ideal for **SaaS founders, consultants, freelancers, and non-US entrepreneurs** who want a US presence with minimal tax burden. Wyoming LLCs are best for cost-conscious founders who value privacy. Delaware LLCs are best for those planning to raise venture capital or who want the strongest legal protections. If you plan to raise institutional funding, consider whether a C-Corp election or conversion may be needed down the line.
