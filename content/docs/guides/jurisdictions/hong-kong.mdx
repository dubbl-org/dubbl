## Overview

Hong Kong is a premier business hub for companies operating in or trading with Asia, particularly mainland China. Its territorial tax system means that profits sourced outside Hong Kong are exempt from tax, making it especially attractive for businesses with offshore income. With a two-tier profits tax system, straightforward incorporation, and no VAT or sales tax, Hong Kong remains one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for international businesses.

## Entity Types Available

- **Private Company Limited by Shares (Ltd)** - The standard structure for most businesses. Minimum one director and one shareholder. The default choice for startups and trading companies
- **Public Company** - For companies intending to list on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange. Minimum two directors and a company secretary
- **Branch Office** - Extension of a foreign company registered in Hong Kong. Not a separate legal entity
- **Representative Office** - For liaison activities only. Cannot conduct business or generate revenue
- **Sole Proprietorship / Partnership** - Simpler structures registered under the Business Registration Ordinance

**The Private Limited Company (Ltd) is the right choice for most founders.**

## Tax Structure

Hong Kong uses a **territorial tax system** - only profits sourced in or derived from Hong Kong are taxable. Offshore profits are exempt, regardless of whether they are remitted to Hong Kong.

### Two-Tier Profits Tax

| Assessable Profits | Rate | Notes |
|--------------------|------|-------|
| First HKD 2,000,000 | 8.25% | Approximately USD 256,000 |
| Above HKD 2,000,000 | 16.5% | Standard rate |

**Unincorporated businesses** (sole proprietors, partnerships) pay 7.5% on the first HKD 2M and 15% thereafter.

**Key points:**

- **Territorial taxation** - profits earned outside Hong Kong are not taxed. This is the single most important feature of the Hong Kong tax system for international businesses
- **No capital gains tax**
- **No withholding tax on dividends** - dividends paid to shareholders (resident or non-resident) are not taxed
- **No VAT or sales tax**
- **No withholding tax on interest** (with limited exceptions for financial institutions)
- **Royalties:** 4.95% withholding on payments to non-residents for use of IP in Hong Kong (30% of the royalty taxed at 16.5%)
- **Foreign-sourced income exemption** must be substantiated - the IRD (Inland Revenue Department) may challenge offshore claims. Maintain proper documentation and substance
- **Tax treaties:** Hong Kong has 40+ comprehensive double taxation agreements

### Offshore Profits Exemption

To claim the offshore profits exemption, you must demonstrate that:

1. The profits-generating activities took place outside Hong Kong
2. Key decisions were made outside Hong Kong
3. Contracts were negotiated, concluded, and executed outside Hong Kong
4. Customers and suppliers are located outside Hong Kong

The IRD reviews offshore claims carefully. Professional tax advice is strongly recommended.

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Choose a company name** and check availability with the Companies Registry
2. **Prepare incorporation documents:** Articles of Association, Form NNC1 (incorporation form), notice of registered office address
3. **Appoint a local company secretary** - must be a Hong Kong resident individual or a Hong Kong-registered company providing secretarial services ($500-2,000/year)
4. **Register with the Companies Registry** - incorporation fee: HKD 1,720 (government fee) plus business registration fee: HKD 2,150/year (one-year certificate) or HKD 5,950 (three-year certificate)
5. **Open a corporate bank account**

**No requirement for a local resident director.** Directors and shareholders can be of any nationality and reside anywhere.

**Minimum share capital:** HKD 1 (no minimum required by law). There is no concept of paid-up capital requirements.

**Total setup cost: $1,500-2,500 USD** including government fees, company secretary, and registered address for the first year.

**Timeline:** 1-4 business days for electronic incorporation. Bank account opening takes 2-6 weeks.

| Hang Seng Bank | Local major bank | HSBC subsidiary, good SME banking products |

| Bank of China (HK) | Local major bank | Useful for China trade connections |
| DBS Hong Kong | Regional bank | Good digital banking experience |
| ZA Bank / Mox / Airstar | Virtual banks | Newer digital banks with easier onboarding |
| Statrys | Neobank | Designed for SMEs, remote onboarding available |
| Wise Business | Multi-currency | Useful for international payments alongside a local account |

**Important:** Hong Kong banks have tightened due diligence requirements significantly. Most traditional banks prefer or require **in-person visits** for account opening. Virtual banks (ZA Bank, Mox) and fintechs (Statrys) offer more accessible remote onboarding. Plan for the bank account opening process to take 2-6 weeks with documentation review.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Annual Return:** Filed with the Companies Registry within 42 days of the anniversary of incorporation. Filing fee: HKD 105
- **Business Registration Renewal:** Annual renewal fee of HKD 2,150 (or paid triennially)
- **Profits Tax Return:** Filed annually with the IRD, typically issued in April. First return issued 18 months after incorporation. Due within 1-3 months of issuance depending on the accounting year end
- **Audit:** **Required for all limited companies** regardless of size or revenue. Financial statements must be audited by a certified public accountant (CPA). This is a significant ongoing cost - budget HKD 5,000-30,000/year depending on complexity
- **Employer's Return:** Filed annually if you have employees (Form BIR56A)
- **Company Secretary:** Must maintain a qualified company secretary at all times
- **Registered Office:** Must maintain a registered office address in Hong Kong
- **Significant Controllers Register (SCR):** Must maintain a register of significant controllers accessible for inspection

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | Territorial taxation - offshore profits are completely exempt from tax |
| Pro | Two-tier system with 8.25% rate on first HKD 2 million of assessable profits |
| Pro | No VAT, no sales tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax on dividends |
| Pro | No local director requirement - fully foreign-owned and managed companies are allowed |
| Pro | Gateway to mainland China - close economic ties and various trade schemes (CEPA) |
| Pro | Strong common law legal system based on English law |
| Con | Mandatory audit for all limited companies - significant annual cost even for small businesses |
| Con | Bank account opening has become increasingly difficult with enhanced due diligence |
| Con | Offshore profits exemption requires careful documentation and may be challenged by the IRD |
| Con | Local company secretary requirement adds ongoing cost |
| Con | Business registration fee (HKD 2,150/year) is an unavoidable annual cost |
| Con | Political and regulatory landscape has shifted - monitor developments carefully |

## Best For

Hong Kong is ideal for **businesses involved in China trade, companies with genuinely offshore income, and founders who need a credible Asia-Pacific base**. It works particularly well for import/export businesses, companies with clients across Asia, e-commerce businesses sourcing from China, and holding companies for regional operations. The territorial tax system makes it especially attractive for businesses where profits are generated outside Hong Kong, as these can be completely tax-exempt with proper structuring and documentation.
