## Overview

A UK Limited Company (Ltd) is one of the fastest and cheapest companies to form in the world. With same-day online incorporation through Companies House, a strong legal framework, and access to the UK's extensive financial ecosystem, a UK Ltd is a compelling choice for founders seeking credibility and EU-adjacent market access. The UK also has a well-developed fintech sector, making it easy to access modern banking and payment infrastructure.

## Entity Types Available

- **Private Limited Company (Ltd)** - The standard structure for most businesses. Limited by shares, with one or more shareholders and directors
- **Limited by Guarantee** - Used for non-profits and community interest companies. No share capital
- **Public Limited Company (PLC)** - For companies intending to offer shares to the public. Minimum £50,000 share capital. Rarely relevant for startups
- **LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)** - Pass-through taxation similar to a US LLC. Requires at least two designated members

## Tax Structure

The UK uses a **corporation tax** system where the company pays tax on its worldwide profits.

| Profit Band | Rate | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| £0 - £50,000 | 19% | Small profits rate |
| £50,001 - £250,000 | 26.5% marginal | Marginal relief applies - effective rate scales between 19% and 25% |
| £250,001+ | 25% | Main rate |

**Key points:**

- The 19% small profits rate applies only to companies with profits under £50,000 (since April 2023, marginal relief replaces the old flat 19% rate)
- **VAT registration** is mandatory once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in a rolling 12-month period. Voluntary registration is available below this threshold
- **No withholding tax** on dividends paid to non-UK shareholders (under domestic law)
- **R&D tax credits** are available for qualifying expenditure, though the scheme was reformed in April 2024
- **Capital gains** are taxed at the standard corporation tax rate
- **Annual Investment Allowance (AIA):** 100% deduction on qualifying capital expenditure up to £1 million

### VAT

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Standard rate | 20% |
| Reduced rate | 5% (certain goods/services) |
| Zero rate | 0% (exports, certain food, books) |
| Registration threshold | £90,000 |
| Flat Rate Scheme | Available for small businesses - simplified VAT accounting |

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Choose a company name** and check availability on Companies House
2. **Register online** at Companies House (gov.uk) - same-day incorporation available
3. **Provide details:** registered office address, director(s), shareholder(s), SIC code, articles of association
4. **Pay the incorporation fee:** £12 online (£30 for paper filing, £30 for same-day service)
5. **Register for Corporation Tax** with HMRC within 3 months of starting business activity
6. **Register for VAT** if applicable
7. **Open a business bank account**

**Total setup cost: £12-500** depending on whether you use an agent or handle it yourself.

**Timeline:** Same-day for online incorporation. HMRC Corporation Tax registration takes 1-2 weeks.

**Non-UK residents:** Can form a UK Ltd remotely. No requirement for a UK-resident director. You will need a UK registered office address (virtual office services available from ~£50/year).

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **Confirmation Statement:** Filed annually with Companies House (£13 online). Confirms company details are up to date
- **Annual Accounts:** Filed with Companies House within 9 months of the financial year end. Micro-entities and small companies can file abbreviated accounts
- **Corporation Tax Return (CT600):** Filed with HMRC within 12 months of the accounting period end. Tax payment due 9 months and 1 day after the period end
- **VAT Returns:** Quarterly if VAT-registered (via Making Tax Digital)
- **Payroll (PAYE):** If you have UK employees, run payroll and report to HMRC in real time (RTI)
- **PSC Register:** Maintain a register of People with Significant Control

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | Same-day online incorporation for just £12 - one of the fastest and cheapest in the world |
| Pro | Strong legal framework and global credibility |
| Pro | No withholding tax on dividends to non-UK shareholders |
| Pro | Excellent fintech ecosystem - easy access to Stripe, payment processors, and modern banking |
| Pro | Marginal relief means effective tax rate below 25% for profits under £250k |
| Pro | EU-adjacent - good base for serving European customers post-Brexit |
| Con | 25% headline corporation tax rate is relatively high compared to some alternatives |
| Con | VAT compliance adds complexity once you exceed £90,000 turnover |
| Con | Companies House records are fully public - director names, addresses, and accounts are visible |
| Con | Post-Brexit, no longer an EU company - may need separate EU entity for some use cases |
| Con | HMRC reporting requirements can be burdensome for small companies |

## Best For

A UK Ltd is ideal for **founders seeking credibility, EU-adjacent market access, and strong fintech infrastructure**. It works well for SaaS businesses serving UK and European customers, companies that need access to UK payment processors, and founders who want a reputable jurisdiction with transparent regulation. Particularly strong for fintech startups due to the FCA regulatory framework and London's financial ecosystem.
