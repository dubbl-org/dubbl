## Overview

Portugal has become one of Europe's most attractive destinations for entrepreneurs, digital nomads, and tech professionals. It combines a moderate corporate tax rate with powerful personal tax incentives, a growing tech ecosystem centered around Lisbon and Porto, and one of the best quality-of-life propositions in Western Europe. The Non-Habitual Resident (NHR) regime, which drew thousands of foreign professionals, was replaced in January 2024 by the Tax Incentive for Scientific Research and Innovation (IFICI), targeting a more specific set of qualifying professionals. Portugal also offers rapid company formation through its Empresa na Hora (Company in an Hour) program and multiple visa pathways for non-EU citizens.

## Entity Types Available

- **Sociedade por Quotas (Lda)** - Private limited company, equivalent to a Ltd or GmbH. The most common structure for SMEs and startups. Requires minimum 1 quotaholder (shareholder)
- **Sociedade Anonima (SA)** - Public limited company, required for larger enterprises or those seeking public listing. Minimum share capital of EUR 50,000 and at least 5 shareholders
- **Unipessoal Lda** - Single-member private limited company, a variant of the Lda for solo founders
- **Sole Proprietorship (Empresario em Nome Individual)** - Simplest structure, but offers no liability separation

## Tax Structure

| Item | Rate |
|------|------|
| Corporate tax (standard CIT - IRC) | 21% |
| Corporate tax (SME reduced rate) | 17% on first EUR 50,000 of taxable income |
| Autonomous taxation surcharges | Vary by expense type (e.g., vehicle costs) |
| Municipal surcharge (Derrama) | Up to 1.5% depending on municipality |
| State surcharge | 3% on profits EUR 1.5m-7.5m, 5% on EUR 7.5m-35m, 9% above EUR 35m |
| IFICI (personal tax for qualifying professionals) | 20% flat rate on employment/self-employment income for 10 years |
| Capital gains tax (personal) | 28% (or progressive rates if opted) |
| VAT (standard rate) | 23% |
| Dividend withholding tax | 25% (reduced under treaties) |

**Key points:**

- The effective corporate tax rate for SMEs can be as low as 17% on the first EUR 50,000 of profits, making Portugal competitive for small businesses
- The IFICI regime (replacing NHR from January 2024) offers a flat 20% personal income tax rate on Portuguese-sourced employment and self-employment income for qualifying professionals in scientific research and innovation fields. It is available for 10 consecutive years to individuals who have not been Portuguese tax residents in the previous 5 years
- Portugal has no wealth tax
- The country has tax treaties with 80+ countries
- Patent Box regime available, offering a 50% exemption on income from patents and certain other IP rights
- SIFIDE II provides R&D tax credits of 32.5% on base expenditure and 50% on incremental expenditure
- Social security contributions for employers are approximately 23.75% of gross salary

## Setup Process and Costs

1. **Obtain a Portuguese tax number (NIF)** - Required for all shareholders and directors. Non-residents need a fiscal representative
2. **Choose the company formation method:**
   - **Empresa na Hora** - Walk-in service at designated registry offices. Choose from pre-approved articles of association, form the company in under an hour. Available at Registos offices nationwide
   - **Traditional incorporation** - More flexibility in articles of association, takes 1-2 weeks
   - **Online via Portal da Empresa** - Digital incorporation option
3. **Deposit share capital** - Minimum EUR 1 per quotaholder for Lda (must be deposited within the first year or as defined in the articles)
4. **Register with Tax Authority (AT)** and Social Security
5. **Open a bank account**

**Minimum share capital:** EUR 1 per quotaholder for Lda

**Total setup cost: $1,000-2,000** including NIF, registration fees, and basic legal support. Empresa na Hora costs approximately EUR 360 in government fees.

**Timeline:** 1 day via Empresa na Hora, 1-4 weeks via traditional process.

## Annual Compliance Requirements

- **IES (Informacao Empresarial Simplificada)** - Simplified annual business information return, combining accounting, tax, and statistical reporting. Due by July 15 for companies with a December year-end
- **IRC tax return (Modelo 22)** - Corporate tax return due by May 31 (for December year-end companies)
- **Monthly/quarterly VAT returns** - Monthly for companies with turnover above EUR 650,000, quarterly below
- **SAF-T accounting file** - Monthly SAF-T (Standard Audit File for Tax) submission to the Tax Authority
- **Statutory audit** - Required for companies meeting 2 of 3 criteria: total assets above EUR 1.5 million, turnover above EUR 3 million, or 50+ employees
- **Social security declarations** - Monthly reporting and contributions for employees
- **Central Beneficiary Register (RCBE)** - Beneficial ownership information must be filed and kept up to date
- **Annual accounts approval** - Shareholder approval of annual accounts within 3 months of year-end

## Visa Options for Non-EU Citizens

| Visa | Purpose | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| D7 (Passive Income) | Retirement, remote workers with stable income | Requires proof of passive or regular income, leads to residency |
| D8 (Digital Nomad) | Remote workers employed by non-Portuguese companies | Requires income of at least 4x Portuguese minimum wage |
| Tech Visa | Startup employees and founders | Simplified process for certified startups and tech companies |
| Startup Visa | Entrepreneurs launching innovative businesses | Requires incubator certification, leads to residency |

## Pros and Cons

| Pro/Con | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Pro | IFICI regime offers 20% flat personal tax for qualifying professionals for 10 years |
| Pro | SME reduced rate of 17% on first EUR 50,000 makes it competitive for small businesses |
| Pro | Empresa na Hora allows same-day company formation |
| Pro | High quality of life, affordable cost of living compared to Western Europe, and warm climate |
| Pro | Growing tech ecosystem with strong talent pool in Lisbon and Porto |
| Pro | Multiple visa pathways (D7, D8, Tech Visa, Startup Visa) for non-EU citizens |
| Con | 21% standard CIT rate is higher than Ireland (12.5%) or Cyprus (12.5%) |
| Con | NHR regime ended - IFICI is narrower and only applies to specific qualifying activities |
| Con | Social security contributions for employers (~23.75%) add significant cost to hiring locally |
| Con | Bureaucracy can be slow, especially at government offices and for non-Portuguese speakers |
| Con | 25% dividend withholding tax unless reduced by treaty |
| Con | Autonomous taxation surcharges on certain expenses (vehicles, representation costs) increase effective tax burden |

## Best For

Portugal is ideal for **tech professionals, digital nomads, and entrepreneurs seeking EU residency with a high quality of life**. The IFICI regime is particularly attractive for qualifying researchers and innovation professionals who can benefit from the 20% flat tax rate. The growing Lisbon and Porto tech scenes, affordable living costs, strong visa programs, and same-day company formation make it a compelling choice for startups and remote businesses. Best suited for founders who want to live in Europe, access the EU single market, and benefit from Portugal's lifestyle advantages while maintaining a tax-efficient personal structure.
