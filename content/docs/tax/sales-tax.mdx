title: Sales Tax

---

dubbl helps you track sales tax obligations for US state and local jurisdictions. Navigate to **Tax > Sales Tax** to view your sales tax summary.

> **Disclaimer:** Sales tax rules vary widely across US states and localities, and change frequently. Always verify rates and nexus requirements with a qualified tax professional. If you notice outdated information, please [open an issue](https://github.com/dubbl-org/dubbl/issues).

## Setup

1. Create tax rates for each jurisdiction where you have nexus (tax obligation)
2. Set the rate percentage and assign a name (e.g., "California Sales Tax - 7.25%")
3. Apply the appropriate tax rate when creating invoices

## Nexus

You may have sales tax obligations in a state if you have:

- Physical presence (office, warehouse, employees)
- Economic nexus (exceeding revenue or transaction thresholds)

Each state sets its own thresholds. Check your state's Department of Revenue for current rules.

## Sales Tax Summary

The sales tax page shows:

- Tax collected by jurisdiction
- Tax period totals
- Outstanding amounts to remit

## Filing

dubbl provides the data needed for your sales tax returns. File through each state's tax authority portal.

## Tips

- Review tax rates periodically as states update their rates
- Track nexus in all states where you sell or have presence
- Set up tax periods to match your filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually)
