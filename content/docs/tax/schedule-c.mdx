Schedule C (Form 1040) is used by US sole proprietors to report business income and expenses. dubbl generates a worksheet that maps your financial data to Schedule C line items. Navigate to **Tax > Schedule C**.

> **Disclaimer:** US tax rules change annually. This worksheet is a tool to help organize your data, not a substitute for professional tax advice. Always verify with a qualified tax professional before filing. If you notice outdated information, please [open an issue](https://github.com/dubbl-org/dubbl/issues).

## How It Works

dubbl maps your chart of accounts to Schedule C categories:

| Schedule C Line | dubbl Source |
|---|---|
| Gross receipts | Revenue accounts (4000-4999) |
| Cost of goods sold | COGS accounts (5000-5999) |
| Advertising | Marketing expense accounts |
| Office expenses | Office expense accounts |
| Rent | Rent expense accounts |
| Utilities | Utility expense accounts |
| Other expenses | Remaining expense accounts |

## Generating the Worksheet

1. Ensure your chart of accounts is mapped to the correct expense categories
2. Navigate to **Tax > Schedule C**
3. Select the tax year
4. Review the generated worksheet

## Using the Data

The worksheet provides organized totals you can transfer to your Schedule C form or share with your tax preparer. It is not a substitute for the official IRS form.
